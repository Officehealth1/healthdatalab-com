Why refunds instead of discounts?

A discount says: “We’ll charge you less because it’s early.”

A refund says: “We’ll charge you the real price — and if you show up and do the work, you earn it back.”

The difference matters. Discounts attract bargain-hunters. Refunds attract practitioners who are serious about using this in their practice. We want the second group.

The refund model creates engagement pressure, not financial pressure. You’re not risking money — you’re committing effort. And that commitment is exactly what makes the course work.

After the founding cohort fills, the course moves to a waiting list at the full £997 price. No refund offers. No discounts. If you’re reading this and the options above are still open — that’s your window.
